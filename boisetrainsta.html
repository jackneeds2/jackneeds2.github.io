The Boise Train Depot is a large Spanish style station built on a spectacular site on a hillside in Boise. The station is anchored at one end by the Idaho State Capitol building and at the other by the Union Pacific station. The depot features a clock and bell tower, a heavy timbered roof over a magnificent two story waiting room, fancy iron grille work and tile mosaic floors. In 1925, the station gardens overlooking Capitol Boulevard were also dedicated. In 1992 began a restoration project turning the station and its magnificent waiting room and other spaces into a museum featuring railroad MK history. With a splendid panoramic view of the Boise Front, the building grounds include Platt Gardens, a lovely park with pathways, a gazebo and a koi fish pond. Depot amenities include The Great Hall, a soaring 3,542-square-foot multi-story atrium that once served as the building's waiting room.
