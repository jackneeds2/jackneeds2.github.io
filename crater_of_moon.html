Crater of the Moon National Preserve is located on the Snake River Plain located in central Idaho. Average elevation of the Preserve is 5,900 feet above sea level. This Preserve features volcanic and represents the best-preserved flood basalt area in the continental United States. The preserve was formed between 15,000 and 2,000 years ago, the Craters of the Moon lava field formed during eight major eruption periods. During these eruptions the Craters lava field would grow to now cover some 618 square miles.
